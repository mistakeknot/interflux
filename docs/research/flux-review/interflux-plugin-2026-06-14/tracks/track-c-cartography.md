source_domain: Renaissance portolan-chart cartographic compilation — a master chartmaker reconciling many pilots' conflicting logs, bearings, and prior charts into one authoritative coastline.
distance_rationale: A pre-modern craft of synthesizing one authoritative artifact from many noisy, conflicting, differently-credible eyewitness sources — far from software. expected_isomorphisms: source-credibility weighting; reconciling contradictory bearings; copying errors forward from prior charts; blank-space-vs-invented-coastline; shared projection/compass-rose drift.

---

The chartmaker's craft is the discipline of *not* believing all reports equally. A pilot who has rounded the cape twenty times outranks a tavern rumor; two ships that independently logged the same shoal from different headings confirm it more strongly than two ships that sailed in convoy and copied each other's logbook; a soft sandbank gets a sounding, a region nobody sailed gets left blank, not invented. interflux's synthesis phase is structurally a chart-compilation engine. Mapped against that craft, here is where it compiles well and where it risks drawing coastline that was never observed.

- **[P1] Convoy bias — convergence counts copied bearings as if they were independent fixes** — `docs/spec/core/synthesis.md:126-141`, `skills/flux-engine/phases/synthesize.md:339-343` — **Mechanism:** the chartmaker's cardinal rule is that two logs only *confirm* a shoal if the two ships sailed independently; two boats in convoy sharing one lookout produce one observation wearing two hats. **Parallel:** interflux's convergence metric (`N/M agents`, High/Medium/Low confidence) treats every additional agent that reports a finding as an independent fix that raises confidence. But review-mode agents are dispatched against the *same sliced content* and largely the *same base model*. Two same-family agents agreeing is a convoy, not two independent fixes. **Insight:** the spec *partially* knows this — the cross-family 1.5x multiplier (`synthesis.md:142-162`) and the cross-section slicing boost (`synthesize.md:341`) are exactly "reward independent headings." But the *base* convergence number, the one rendered as "High confidence — multiple perspectives agree" in `summary.md` (`synthesis.md:314-316`), still counts convoy agreement at face value. The honest move is to make weighted_convergence (not raw count) drive the confidence *label*, so "3/4 agents, all Claude, all on the same slice" never prints as "High."

- **[P0] Invented coastline — the synthesis subagent is the single pilot whose log nobody cross-checks** — `skills/flux-engine/phases/synthesize.md:45-108` — **Mechanism:** every prior chart is itself a synthesis of earlier reports; the danger is the compiler who quietly "fairs the curve" between two contradictory soundings, drawing a smooth confident coastline through water no ship actually sounded. **Parallel:** Step 3.2 delegates ALL collection, dedup, conflict detection, verdict-writing and prose generation to one intersynth subagent, and instructs the host "Do NOT read agent output files yourself... The host agent never touched any individual agent output file" (`synthesize.md:46-47, 66, 102`). The synthesis prose in `summary.md` is therefore an un-cross-checked rendering by a single haiku-tier agent (`agent-roles.yaml:172` — "synthesis is structuring"). Nothing verifies that a P0 in the summary actually traces to a P0 in an agent file, or that a smoothed-over "Key Findings" sentence didn't blend two agents into a claim neither made. **Insight/risk:** this is the chart-of-charts hazard — a confident final artifact whose fidelity to the source soundings is asserted, not checked. The contract has the right primitive (the Findings Index is machine-parseable, `shared-contracts.md:11-16`); the missing control is a cheap structural diff: assert that every finding ID and severity in `findings.json` is grounded in a parsed Findings Index line, and flag any summary claim with no backing index entry as invented coastline.

- **[P1] Trusting the cabin boy on a P0 — credibility weighting is binary and exists only at the provider boundary** — `docs/spec/core/synthesis.md:164-166`, `config/flux-drive/model-registry.yaml:1-7` — **Mechanism:** the chartmaker weights a sounding by *who took it and under what conditions* — a leadsman in fog reports differently than a master in clear weather — and weights *more* skeptically when a single low-credibility source makes an extraordinary claim (a reef where charts show open sea). **Parallel:** interflux has exactly one credibility axis: `model_family` top-tier (claude) vs standard (openrouter), and it fires only for single-source P0/P1 from non-top-tier providers → `verification_recommended: true` (`synthesis.md:164-166`). **Insight:** the weighting is right in spirit but coarse. There is no per-*agent* credibility (fd-safety on a security P0 is a master-in-clear-weather; the same agent opining outside its domain is a cabin boy guessing) and no notion of an *extraordinary-claim* threshold — a lone P0 that contradicts every other agent's "safe" verdict should be weighted more skeptically than a lone P0 in an area nobody else examined. Today both are just "convergence: 1, confidence: low." The chartmaker would treat "lone claim *against* consensus" and "lone claim *in unexplored water*" as different epistemic situations; interflux collapses them.

- **[P2] Contradictory bearings are preserved but never reconciled — the chart shows two coastlines and ships into the gap** — `docs/spec/core/synthesis.md:80-93` (Rules 4-5) — **Mechanism:** when two pilots report the cape at different latitudes the chartmaker must *decide* which to trust (or sail out and resound), because a chart that draws both lines is unusable for navigation. **Parallel:** interflux's Rule 4 (conflicting severity → use highest) is a genuine reconciliation, but Rule 5 (conflicting recommendations → "preserve both with attribution... Do NOT pick a winner — the user decides," `synthesis.md:87-92`) explicitly *declines* to reconcile. **Insight/risk:** for *recommendations* this is defensible (the user is the navigator). The structural gap is that there is no machinery to surface *factual* contradictions — two agents asserting incompatible claims about what the code *does* (not what to do about it). Rule 5 dedups by title similarity and shelves the disagreement; it never escalates "these two agents describe reality differently" as its own finding warranting a tie-break (a re-read, a third agent, or a research escalation). Contradictory soundings about the terrain itself, not just the route, deserve a resound, not a footnote.

- **[P1] Shared compass-rose drift — every source can be wrong the same way and convergence will bless it** — `docs/spec/core/synthesis.md:142-162` — **Mechanism:** if every pilot used the same miscalibrated compass with the same magnetic deviation, all their bearings agree *and all are wrong by the same angle*; convergence is highest precisely where the systematic error is shared. This is the failure that destroys naive cross-checking. **Parallel:** interflux dispatches agents that, in the default single-provider deployment, share one base model (claude). A blind spot or hallucination tendency baked into that model is a shared compass-rose: all agents drift the same direction, agree strongly, and convergence reports "High confidence." **Insight:** the cross-family multiplier (`synthesis.md:150-162`) is the *correct and only* defense in the codebase — it is literally "trust agreement more when the compasses are different." The risk is that it is opt-in and rare: most real runs are single-family (the field collapses to 1, multiplier no-ops, per `synthesis.md:162`). The chartmaker's lesson is that single-compass convergence is *suspicious*, not reassuring. interflux should surface a standing caveat whenever `cross_family_convergence == 1` across the whole run — "all findings from one model family; agreement may reflect shared bias" — rather than silently letting the no-op pass as if independence were achieved.

- **[P2] Foreign-chart contamination is guarded; stale prior charts are not** — `skills/flux-engine/phases/synthesize.md:27-32` (quire-mark / run-uuid), `skills/flux-engine/phases/synthesize.md:592-596` (decay check) — **Mechanism:** two distinct chartroom hazards: (a) accidentally binding another expedition's chart sheet into your atlas, and (b) faithfully copying a 50-year-old chart whose coastline has since silted up. **Parallel:** interflux defends (a) rigorously — the run-uuid quire-mark rejects "Foreign" files from prior/concurrent runs (`synthesize.md:27-32`), an excellent provenance control. For (b), the compounding step injects prior knowledge entries into future agents and has a decay check that archives entries unconfirmed in >60 days (`synthesize.md:592-596`). **Insight/risk:** the decay control exists but is *time-based only*. A knowledge entry can be re-confirmed by an agent that was *primed* with it — the spec even names this hazard ("primed confirmation" vs "independently confirmed," `synthesize.md:578-583`) and correctly refuses to refresh `lastConfirmed` on primed confirmations. That is the right rule, but it depends entirely on the priming agent honestly self-reporting which kind of confirmation it made. A chartmaker doesn't ask the copyist "did you actually sail there?" and trust the answer. The structural weakness: error-propagation resistance hinges on agent self-attestation of independence, with no external check that a "confirmed" prior finding was re-derived rather than re-copied.

---

**Net assessment for the synthesizer:** interflux's compilation engine has the chartmaker's *vocabulary* — provenance marks (run-uuid), independence rewards (cross-family/cross-section), blank-space honesty (single-source flagged "verify independently"), and refusal to invent winners on recommendations. Where it is most exposed, in chartmaking terms: (1) it lets convoy agreement read as independent confirmation in the user-facing confidence label; (2) the chart-of-charts compiler (intersynth) is a single un-cross-checked hand whose summary prose is asserted-faithful, not verified against soundings; and (3) its defenses against shared-compass drift and copied-forward errors both exist but are opt-in or self-attested rather than enforced. The highest-leverage single fix is the structural grounding check on the synthesis subagent's output (P0 above) — it is the chartmaker's most basic discipline (every line on the chart traces to a sounding) and the one interflux currently takes on faith.
